Getting Started with Google Business Profile

Are you a local business owner with your search history filled with searches like “How to increase my business sales?”, “Why is my business not showing in Google?”, and “How to get listed on the Google search engine?” To answer all these questions, you must find the errors you made when starting your business. Though your business services are curated properly and provide high value to your customers, your online presence cannot be neglected when you try to scale your store. There are a few mistakes which block your potential customer from finding you in their Google searches. Identify the potential issue you made, and get it fixed to boost your online presence.

Improper or No Google Business Profile

Your GBP works as the digital storefront for your business — the very first thing customers see when they search for your business or a related service on Google or Google Maps.

Not having a GBP is like operating a store without a sign, making your business practically invisible. An improper or incomplete GBP pushes your listing to the bottom of Google search results, letting competitors take the lead.

Create your GBP, claim and verify it, and keep it updated regularly to secure your spot in Google search.

Inconsistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone Number)

Your business contact details — name, address, and phone number (NAP) — are the primary way customers connect with you. When these details are inconsistent across platforms like Google, Facebook, your website, and LinkedIn, it not only looks unprofessional but also harms your credibility.

Such inconsistencies can also reduce your chances of appearing in local search results, as Google values accurate and reliable information.

Use accurate, up-to-date contact details and keep them consistent across all platforms. This makes it easier for customers to reach you and boosts your trustworthiness in Google’s eyes.

Lack of GBP Updates and Posts

A Google Business Profile isn’t a one-time setup — it’s an ongoing effort. Simply creating a profile isn’t enough; you need to keep it updated with the latest information, along with fresh posts and engaging visuals about your business, to attract more views from potential customers.

An up-to-date profile with frequent, relevant posts is just as important as having accurate details. Google’s algorithm favors recently updated and active profiles, giving them higher visibility in search results.

Poor Review Management

Reviews play a vital role in converting customers from simply viewing your profile to actually visiting your store or showing interest in your services.

Even if your business ranks at the top of search results, potential customers will still check reviews and feedback before choosing you over competitors. A GBP profile with a high volume of genuine reviews builds trust and attracts organic traffic to your brand.

It’s not just about collecting reviews — responding to them is equally important. Thoughtful replies show you value customer feedback and demonstrate the quality of your customer service.

No Local or Technical SEO

Creating and maintaining an updated profile alone won’t guarantee a top spot in search results — especially with strong competition. To outrank others, your profile needs to be fully optimized for local SEO.

Don’t rely solely on your Google Business Profile. Drive customers through multiple channels like your website and blog. To get these listed at the top of Google search, they must be technically SEO-optimized, with the right keywords, structure, and speed.

A well-optimized digital presence increases your visibility across platforms and attracts customers from multiple sources, not just Google Maps.

No Website or Backlink Strategy

Your website is a crucial part of your Google Business Profile. Without it, your profile appears incomplete and weaker in Google’s algorithm, which can push it lower in search results.

A well-designed website that clearly describes your business and services works like a digital brochure, helping potential customers understand you better and increasing the chances they’ll choose you over competitors.

Linking your website across multiple platforms ensures customers can find you through various channels — not just Google — ultimately increasing your reach and customer count.

Lack of Mobile Optimization

Most potential customers search for services on the go using their mobile phones, making a mobile-friendly website essential for connecting with them effectively.

A site that loads quickly, adapts to any screen size, and is easy to navigate keeps visitors engaged and reduces the chance they’ll leave for a competitor. Google also prioritizes mobile-friendly sites in search rankings, meaning better visibility for your business.

Being found — and offering a smooth browsing experience — right when and where your customers are searching gives you a clear edge over competitors.
